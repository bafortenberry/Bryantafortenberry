From hangtime paper airplane contests to breaking ankles skating churchyards, his fascination with the geeks to the streets was cemented forever in his youth.

Enduring a cross-country move in his early teens, a quaint town birthed a harrowing fear of complacency and set him on a path towards greatness. Today, he is a student of the arts and psychology. His mornings consist of The New Yorker, over-easy eggs, and an excuse not to run.

At the University of Alabama, he gathered a plethora of unique experiences from engineering, geology, and business to creative advertising. He has worked with one golf course, two oil & gas companies, two professional sports organizations, and three record labels.
